"Along the River During the Qingming Festival"

 To commemorate the 90th Anniversary of the Palace Museum and spread Chinese traditional culture throughout the world, American Gold Coin Inc. is proud to produce the first set of limited edition gold coins featuring a detailed reproduction of the national treasure "Along the River During the Qingming Festival", an ink painting by the Song Dynasty artist Zhang Zeduan. For the first time, the peerless elegance of the scroll is reproduced in flawless miniature.


 Known as the First Chinese Treasure, the scroll "Along the River During the Qingming Festival" is the most precious piece in the Palace Museum. Hailed as "China's Mona Lisa", the scroll is valued as one of the world's three top-class cultural treasures along with Da Vinci's "Mona Lisa" and the Rosetta Stone.


 This set of 3 commemorative coins are forged with subtle-relief mirror sandblasting. Each coin is 18cm in diameter, with 99.99% gold and 99.99% silver at a ratio of 1:10. The complete collection has a total denomination value of up to 600USD. The back of the coins feature a motif of American bald eagles, while the front is engraved with the full details of the original scroll, making the set a world-class collectable treasure.
